RHINOCEROS

Scientific name: Rhinocerotidae
Estimated population in wild: 27,000
Decline: In the 20th century there were an estimated 500,000 rhinos and this has declined to around 27,000 rhinos in recent times
Life span:35-50 years varying between rhino species
Location: Asia and Africa. Rhinos have gone extinct in many areas of their original habitat
IUCN status: Black Rhino - Critically endangered (2020), status varies depending on species, but all remain around the endangered region.
Threatened by: Poaching for the illegal horn trade. Alongside habitat loss. Over the last 10 years, 7,100 African rhinos have been killed by poaching, with nearly 2 rhinos a day

Fun fact: Rhinos have poor vision yet communicate in many unique ways, through their faeces, and an array of funny noises such as snorting when angry, and sneezes as alarms and screams.

AFRICAN WILD DOG

Scientific name: Lycaon pictus
Estimated population in wild: 6,600 individuals
Decline: Decreasing
Life span: 10 years
Location: Sub-Saharan Africa
IUCN status: Endangered (2012)
Threatened by: Expansion of humans alongside habitat fragmentation. Alongside conflict with humans and hunting for meat
Fun fact: They have a high level of communication which provides them an 80% success rate when hunting. The winnings are given to pups as they have a higher priority than other members of the pack. African wild dogs give birth in abandoned, underground animal dens.

ELEPHANT

Scientific name: Loxodonta
Estimated population in wild: 415,000
Decline: Significant decline, an estimated 111,000 elephants were lost in a decade from 2006 to 2016
Life span: 60 - 70 years
Location: Mainly savannas, grasslands, and forests, however, inhabit a wide range of locations
IUCN status: Overall endangered and/or vulnerable. Asian Elephant - Endangered (2020)
Threatened by: Illegal ivory trade and habitat loss resulting in human conflict

Fun fact: Elephant species are distinguished by their ears and have up to roughly 150,000 muscles in their trunk, and can hold up to 8 litres of water. Their trunks are incredibly versatile.

WILDEBEEST

Scientific name: Connochaetes
Estimated population in wild: around 1,500,000
Decline: Increasing due to the increase of the populations in Serengeti National Park
Life span: 20 years
Location: Open woodlands and grassy plains.
IUCN status: Least concern (2020)
Threatened by: Habitat fragmentation which occurs when areas are fenced off for agricultural purposes.
Fun fact: They migrate 1,600 kilometres over a year.

PRAIRIE DOG

Scientific name: Cynomys
Estimated population in wild: 10-20 million
Decline: Estimated decline of 95%, and a continuing decline.
Life span: 3 - 4 years
Location: Grasslands and savannas mainly in the United States such as Utah.
IUCN status: Mainly endangered.
Threatened by: Poisoning and shooting by humans, habitat fragmentation, and the loss of habitat. Farmers view them as pests and attempt to eradicate the species.

Fun fact: They are highly intelligent and have unique barks for different situations. They are also crucial to maintaining biodiversity due to their burrowing nature which often provides homes for other wildlife.

CHEETAH

Scientific name: Acinonyx jubatus
Estimated population in wild: 7,100
Decline: Decreasing, data showing a 50% decrease over the past 40 years and nearly extinct within Asia.
Life span: 12 years
Location: Southern ranges of Africa and the Sahara Desert
IUCN status: Vulnerable (2022)
Threatened by: Habitat loss and climate change. Hunted by farmers due to their nature to attack livestock. There is also the belief domestic cats pass diseases to cheetahs.

Fun fact: They are the world’s fastest land animal, being able to reach 70km/h in 3 seconds. They also only purr and meow not roar.

ZEBRA

Scientific name: Equus quagga
Estimated population in wild: 300,000
Decline: Estimated 50% decline in the past 18 years in Grevy Zebras and a 94% decline since the 1980s.
Life span: 20 in the wild, 40 in zoos
Location: Spread across Southern and Eastern Africa, with an estimated 150,000 Plains zebra on the Serengeti-Mara Plains.
IUCN status: Plains Zebra - Near threatened (2016), Grevy’s Zebra - Endangered
Threatened by: Human activities such as hunting and habitat loss due to our agricultural practices.

Fun fact: Each zebra’s stripes are unique, and the stripes' purpose is to regulate body temperatures, the black absorbing the sunlight and the white reflecting it. They communicate non-verbally by using ear movements and facial expressions.

LION

Scientific name: Panthera leo
Estimated population in wild: 20,000
Decline: 43% decline in the last 2 decades.
Life span: up to 16 years
Location: Around Africa, however, there is one small population in India. They have been in Asia and Europe however has gone extinct in those regions.
IUCN status: Vulnerable (2016)
Threatened by: Habitat loss, hunting, and poaching for trophies and conflict

Fun fact: They stay in groups of 4-38 individuals which is called a pride, the average amount is 15. Lions are rather lazy and sleep and/or rest 21-22 hours a day. Their manes are big to intimidate and attract mates and cubs are spotty that become sandy as they age.
