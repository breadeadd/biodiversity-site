MAKE A CHANGE

Be the change you want to see, here are some of the ways you can help the biodiversity of our planet. It’s as simple as altering your lifestyle, being more aware, and even donating to organisations. You have read some facts about the animals we live alongside, and discovered the beauty of their existence, now let’s help them in our own way and give back to their roles in maintaining the environment!

Together we can make a difference!

LIVE LOCAL

Source your foods locally as it saves our environment.
How?
By reducing the amount of greenhouse gases it takes for food to reach our plates, therefore reducing our carbon footprint. A bonus is that it supports local farmers and the economy, and it's fresh!
Plan local flowers, fruits, and vegetables, this shows support to your local environment and wildlife, aiding in preserving biodiversity in our own ecosystems and backyards.

  • Buy local food
  • Buy plants locally
  • Buy local fish
  • Buy local goods!
  • Plant local fruits, vegetables, and flowers

WILDLIFE

Our wildlife is integral in maintaining and preserving our biodiversity, and you can start in your own backyard. By tending to our bees, birds, and local wildlife and ecosystems we can help maintain our biodiversity. Research pesticides and ensure they do not harm your local wildlife, like bees. Bees are integral to our biodiversity and it's as simple as planting nectar-producing plants or going the mile further by making bee boxes. Even our birds are important to our environment as seed dispersal, pollination and provide control for insects and rodents

  • Stay on the track when hiking or walking
  • Take care of our bees
  • Tend to our birds
  • Plant nectar-producing flowers

LIFESTYLE

These small changes in your lifestyle build up to make a big impact in sustaining our biodiversity. Invest in some reusable products so you can use them over and over again, decrease the time of showers and strive to save water. Leaning towards a greener lifestyle not only benefits us but our environment too, walking places can get you fit AND save the world! Being mindful of the resources you use can go a long way!

  • Reduce your carbon footprint
  • Walk instead of driving places
  • Use alternate transport
  • Keep your showers short
  • Reduce your waste, try using eco-friendly products such as reusable cutlery, straws, and cups.

SUSTAINABLE CONSUMERISM

Know where your products come from, and consume sustainably. Make sure purchases come from sustainable and reputable companies. This is especially relevant when travelling to ensure your souvenirs are sustainable and don’t harm the local environment.
Make sure to question, is it made of animal? Or endangered? Don’t buy it so you encourage suppliers to turn to sustainable and legal products.
Marine life is often under threat by overfishing, many fisheries exploit our marine life, avoid endangered fish and look out for an MSC label

  • Research products before you buy them
  • Ask the question, is it made of animal? Is it sustainable?
  • Aim to buy sustainable seafood/meat/plants - look for the MSC label!
